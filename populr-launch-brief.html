Populr — Internal Launch Brief · v1

The social layer
for real life.

Populr is a single iOS + Web app that turns every kind of gathering — a backyard cookout, a neighborhood parade, a recurring yoga class, a sold-out concert — into something people actually remember. It's an invitation platform, a group memory, and a discovery feed, stitched together.

Platforms: iOS · Web (Android port: next) Stack: Expo · Supabase · Cloudflare Status: Feature-complete for v1 launch; App Store submission in progress Working title: populrevents.app

01What Populr actually is

Most event apps pick one lane: invitations (Paperless Post, Partiful), ticketing (Eventbrite, Dice), or local discovery (Meetup, Facebook Events). Populr collapses all three into a single dark-mode, TikTok-feeling experience — and adds a fourth thing no one has nailed yet: the post-event memory.

Before the event

Create an event in 3 steps. Invite by link, SMS, or email. Guests RSVP with one tap — no account required. Discover what's happening around you, filtered by interests.

During the event

Live group chat. Host announcements. Potluck / "who's bringing what." A camera FAB that captures Moments — auto-moderated photos and videos that build a shared feed in real time.

After the event

The Moments strip becomes a keepsake. Like, react, rewatch. Your calendar syncs backward to show every event you've been part of. Recurring events build continuity across weeks and months.

The hook for every marketing conversation: Populr is the only app that stays useful the morning after.

Who it's for

  • Primary: 22–45 social organizers — the friend who's always hosting, the parent running the birthday, the community lead running the block party or yoga class.
  • Secondary: Event discoverers — people who moved to a new city, people looking for weekend plans, people who follow a local venue or band.
  • Tertiary (later): Small-business hosts — studios, chefs, tour guides who repeatedly host ticketed or semi-public events and need a lightweight CRM for their regulars.

02The product, screen by screen

These are the six screens marketing will lead with in the App Store. Each one carries a headline tagline and a set of feature depth beneath the surface. Use the hooks under each screen as starting points for copy, social cuts, and press pitch angles.

Event Detail · The hero screen

"Your event. Your vibe."

Full-bleed cover photo, event meta collapsed into a clean stack, a right-side FAB rail (chat, menu, camera with review badge, follow, heart, guest count), and the Moments strip peeking up from below — shared photos & videos from the gathering, live.

Feature depth

  • Single-source guest count circle — green (going) / yellow (maybe) / blue (no RSVPs yet). Organizer tension in one glance.
  • RSVP pill dropdown in the meta row — Going / Maybe / Can't make it, live-updated across all attendees.
  • Realtime group chat (Supabase Realtime) — threaded, persistent, tied to the event, not a separate DM surface.
  • Host Updates — broadcast announcements appear as stacked cards over the hero, disappear after read.
  • Moderator Protections — every uploaded Moment runs through Cloudinary AI Vision (NSFW, violence, weapons). Host sees a review badge on the camera FAB when items need approval.

Marketing hooks

  • "Your party has a URL now." (Share sheet demo.)
  • "The only event app where guests actually want to show up early." (Moments strip teases energy.)
  • "Not a group chat. Not an invite. Both, plus what happens next."
Home Feed · The daily surface

"Everything happening, one place."

Personalized greeting, a gradient "What's happening" headline, stat chips (Upcoming / Pending RSVPs / My Events), filter chips (All / Upcoming / Hosting / Invited / Past), and a vertical card feed. Each card shows cover, title, time, location, guest avatars, and live RSVP status.

Feature depth

  • Badge system: HOST / Private / Public / Recurring / # of Moments / # of Updates — a card tells the full story at a glance.
  • Past events filter — a scrollable history of where you've been. This is the "ambient memory" hook.
  • AdMob banners every 5th card — monetization is already wired.
  • Pull-to-refresh + skeleton states — no jank, no blank screens ever.

Marketing hooks

Moments · The defensible feature

"Moments worth reliving."

A grid of photos and videos contributed by every attendee, auto-flagged NEW since your last visit, filterable (All / Photos / Videos / Mine). Tap opens a vertical, swipeable full-screen player with reactions, counts, and the uploader's avatar.

Feature depth

  • Cloudinary pipeline — upload, AI moderation, CDN, derived thumbnail URLs. Every media asset gets a device-local thumbnail for instant grid loading.
  • Tiered video prefetch (4 tiers: event detail → strip → sheet → player look-ahead) — swipes feel instant, not loading-spinner-y.
  • Engagement analytics — view count, reactions, video completion rate, top uploader. Tracked in PostHog, surfaced in-app as "X views · Y reactions."
  • Organizer moderation queue — anything flagged sits in a sheet for approve/remove before it's visible to guests.

Marketing hooks

Discover · The growth surface

"Discover what's near you."

Location-aware feed ("Near Dallas, TX"), date filter chips (Today / This Week / This Month / Past), two sections: Near You (horizontal scroll of closest concerts, sports, comedy, shows) and All Events (vertical cards with distance, venue, affiliate CTAs).

Feature depth

  • SerpAPI + Ticketmaster ingestion via Supabase Edge Functions — real events from real sources, not a shell directory.
  • Interest categories on profile — the feed is weighted to what you've told us you like (music, comedy, sports, parades, food, fitness…).
  • Affiliate links on every third-party event — if a user buys a ticket through Populr, we get paid. Revenue is wired, ready to scale.
  • "Weekend Picks" push notifications — Thursday 10am cron, one push per user with the best weekend event within ~80 km filtered by their interests.

Calendar · The retention surface

"Your calendar, connected."

Full-month grid with colored dots (host / going / maybe / invited), swipe between months, tap any day to drop into an agenda list. Each agenda card carries a left color bar (gradient for HOST, solid for others) and an uppercase RSVP pill.

Feature depth

  • Recurring events expansion — a weekly yoga class renders on every correct date, not just the start date.
  • Unified view across roles — events you host, events you RSVP'd to, events you were invited to, all in one grid.
  • Pull-to-refresh + error state with retry — built like a production app, not a prototype.

Marketing hooks

Create Event · The low-friction core

"Any schedule. Any event."

A 3-step wizard: Tell us about your event (name, description, type) → when & where → cover photo & privacy. Event type picker handles the hardest part of most event apps: single vs. recurring vs. series — a parade, a weekly yoga class, or a three-night film festival, all modeled correctly.

03What's under the hood (not in the screenshots)

These features don't get App Store shots but matter for the narrative — especially for press, parents, and policy-minded reviewers.

Frictionless guest RSVP

Invite links open a branded, animated landing page (Lifemark bloom + Ken Burns hero + confetti) served by Cloudflare Worker. Guests with no account can RSVP via phone + OTP in under 30 seconds. No download wall.

Moderator Protections

A coined umbrella term for our whole content-safety stack: Cloudinary AI Vision on every Moment, organizer approval queues for flagged content, reporting tools, and planned age gates (under-13 blocked, 13–17 sandboxed with parental consent, adult and family accounts). This is a press angle, not a backend detail.

Invite lifecycle intelligence

Status badges for every invite: pending · sent · viewed · responded · declined. Organizers see who opened their invite and when — Apple-Mail-style read receipts for parties. Resend, swipe-to-delete, re-invite.

Recurring events done right

Full RRULE-style recurrence: "Going to all" / "Going to this one" / "Can't make it this week." Each occurrence has its own guest list, its own chat, its own Moments. Competitors fake this with duplicated events; we model it.

Cross-platform parity

The same codebase ships to iOS, Web, and (next) Android. The web version is served by Cloudflare Workers with HTMLRewriter-injected OG tags, AdSense, and GA4. Every shareable link has a rich preview on iMessage, Slack, WhatsApp, and Twitter.

Analytics & product intelligence

PostHog on every screen (native) + GA4 on web. Event taxonomy already captures: event_viewed, rsvp_submitted, invite_sent, moment_player_opened, moment_viewed, moment_video_completed, moment_reaction, affiliate_clicked. We can answer launch-week questions from day one.

Engagement-weighted discovery (planned)

Static interest categories evolve into a signal-weighted user profile: views, likes, RSVPs, saves, shares all feed a nightly pg_cron recomputation of per-user category weights. This becomes the ranking layer for Weekend Picks and the "For You" section.

How to position against each

vs. Partiful

Partiful owns the "cool party invite" aesthetic but stops at RSVP. Populr has the same invite vibe and then keeps going — chat, Moments, recurring events, discovery. "Partiful invites you. Populr keeps the party going."

vs. Luma

Luma owns tech-meetup calendar culture on web. Populr is mobile-first, social-first, and designed for gatherings that aren't conferences. "Luma is for your Notion-using coworkers. Populr is for your friends."

vs. Facebook Events

Facebook Events is a zombie product — declining engagement, no mobile love, no trust with under-35s. Populr is the clean-slate replacement for people who left FB years ago but still need a party RSVP tool. "The events tab your friends actually open."

vs. Eventbrite

Eventbrite is built for paid tickets and has no social layer. Populr covers the long tail of free and semi-public events, and links out to Eventbrite / Ticketmaster via affiliate when appropriate. "We're not fighting them — we're the front door."

05Technical moat

Most of this doesn't matter in consumer copy, but it matters for investor conversations, press on the "how" side, and hiring.

One codebase, three platforms

Expo SDK 54 + React Native 0.81 + React 19 ships to iOS, Android, and Web from a single TypeScript strict codebase. NativeWind (Tailwind) for styling, no StyleSheet.create. Adds a platform as a marginal, not a step cost.

Supabase as the whole backend

Auth (Apple, Google, phone OTP via Twilio), Postgres with strict RLS on every table, Storage for avatars and thumbnails, Realtime for chat, Edge Functions for SerpAPI and email. No dedicated backend team required.

Cloudflare edge layer

The web version isn't just a PWA — it's a Cloudflare Worker that injects OG tags per event, serves animated invite landings, mediates privacy (public/invite-only/private), and acts as a fallback OG image renderer. First-paint on shared links is sub-100ms globally.

Cloudinary + AI moderation pipeline

Every image or video uploaded to a Moment hits Cloudinary, runs through AI Vision (NSFW / violence / weapons), and only then renders to the feed. Organizers see flagged items in a review queue. This is a full content-safety stack, not a disclaimer on a terms page.

Analytics as a first-class citizen

PostHog (native) + GA4 (web) + affiliate click tracking + AdMob / AdSense. Every revenue event is instrumented. Every engagement event is instrumented. We can measure moment-by-moment from day one.

Testability

testIDs on every interactive element (enforced by CLAUDE.md convention). Jest + React Native Testing Library set up. Maestro and Playwright scaffolds in place for mobile and web end-to-end testing.

1. AdMob banners (iOS) · AdSense (Web) — Live

Every 5th card in the Home Feed, banner on Event Detail, banner on Public Event page. Zero impact on organizer-created flows. Turned off inside private events by design.

2. Affiliate links on discovered events — Live

Ticketmaster, Eventbrite, SerpAPI-surfaced events all carry affiliate CTAs. Every click tracked. Zero incremental work to add more partners — it's a field on the event model.

3. Host-owned affiliate links — Live

A host can attach their own ticket / Venmo / merch link to any event they create. Clicks tracked. We monetize both sides of the marketplace.

4. Premium subscriptions (RevenueCat) — Wired, hidden

react-native-purchases integrated and tested. Ready to flip on for Pro tier (larger events, custom branding, private Moments retention, advanced analytics). Planned for Sprint 12 / post-launch.

The monetization story for launch: "Revenue starts on day one, compounds on week eight." Ads + affiliate are live from the moment the App Store flips us on. Subscriptions roll out after we have retention data.

09Prompts to fuel the marketing discussion

  1. Lead with hosts or guests? Hosts are the acquisition lever (they bring guests for free). Guests are the reason the product feels alive. Which persona do we front-load in ads and PR?
  2. Is Moments the star, or the supporting act? Moments is arguably the most defensible feature we have. But leading with it assumes users already get the event-hosting layer. Do we tease Moments in App Store shot 1 or make it the reveal?
  3. Who do we pitch to press first? Three viable angles: (a) "Partiful killer for millennials," (b) "Safer social photo-sharing for families," (c) "The end of the Google Calendar social-life split." Pick one.
  4. Launch geography: nationwide or city-by-city? Discover is only as good as the SerpAPI + Ticketmaster data in a given metro. Dallas (our seed market) is strong. Do we pick 3–5 launch cities and go deep, or open everywhere and lean on organic?
  5. How loud do we go on content moderation? Moderator Protections is a real, differentiating stack. Leading with it appeals to parents and press; it risks making us sound like a youth-safety product, not a fun one. Decision point.
  6. Price signal on launch day? The app is free with ads. RevenueCat is wired but hidden. Do we tease a Pro tier in launch copy ("Free forever, Pro coming soon") or stay silent until we have retention data?
  7. What single metric do we pick as the north star for launch month? Options: events created per user, invites-sent-to-RSVPs-received ratio, Moments uploaded per event, W1 retention. The instrumentation supports any of them. Pick one per marketing sub-team.
  8. What's our "remember this name" moment? Every launched app has one. Superhuman had the concierge onboarding. Partiful had the vaporwave aesthetic. What's ours? Candidates: the animated invite link, the Moments grid filling up live during a demo, the gradient Lifemark.
